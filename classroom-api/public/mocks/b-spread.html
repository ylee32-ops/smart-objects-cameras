The lecture, now in motion

The class is a verb, not a room.

At 16:48 the room is in lecture mode, ten minutes into the second hour. The smart stage holds the slide; Forest's canopy is pulsing gentle green; Gus is at home, ready, scheduled to enter at break. Seren keeps the entry music low. The gesture timer is armed but quiet.

In twelve minutes the class moves into activity, three groups quadrant-mapping their interaction sketches. The room has cleared its throat for that — focus beam dim, gesture timer about to count down ten — but it has not begun.

All of this is happening at the same time, which is why the page reads more like a magazine of the moment than a list of switches. The room is doing many things at once; the page lets you see them all at once.

Three sketches, one developed.

Pick one corner of the Wendy Ju quadrant and fill it with three plausible smart-classroom interactions. One sketch should go to fidelity — name the signifier, the feedback, the failure mode.

  • Names a beneficiary and a possible harm.
