Interaction Grammar

One board-first language for tags, projection, phone, and cameras.

Use this as the student contract: every object should declare what it affords, every implicit inference should show feedback before it commits, and the projector is the user-facing truth.

The Five Rules

HandlesTags are movable handles for identity, control, and calibration. If students can touch it, it should respond visibly.
PlacesZones, sticky notes, writing clusters, and polygons are places on the board. Projection should land in the same place the student sees.
CommitAction is the general commit/control tag. Dragging it over a valid target shows green drop affordances before anything irreversible happens.
AnswerProjection answers back with glow, fill, pulse, slide change, video state, or classroom-screen summary within one beat.
ExplainThe phone and Figurate explain what the system sees, start live conversation, and convert simple text into room commands.
Explicit

Student Says

Move a tag, rotate a tag, drag Action, draw a zone, tap the phone lens, ask Figurate, or press a quick phone control.

Implicit

Room Infers

Detect tag pose, sticky-note color, writing clusters, QR targets, pose/hand gestures, or object hints. Inference must be conservative.

Feedback

Room Responds

Pulse the tag, draw the mapped projection, show green acceptable drop zones, update classroom slides, or put Figurate response on the classroom projector.

Tag Set

CalibrationFixed board corners. These stay when tags are cleared.
ZoneDefines an area. A second diagonal Zone resizes it.
ActionContextual commit: capture zone, play video, change slide, erase/write.
FocusProjects a white highlighter; rotation changes radius.
Write / EraseWriting mode and eraser behavior, backed by board strokes and OCR later.
Slide / VideoProjects media beside the tag; Action controls state.
Vertex / 3DVertices close into filled polygons; 3D rotation changes shape.
FigurateCharacter presence for room-aware questions and phone Live.

Do

Make Affordances Visible

Green means a valid drop target. White glow means focus/highlight. Yellow means pending/thinking. Blue/cyan means informational overlay. Pulses mean the room saw movement or action.

Do Not

Hide the System State

Do not make projection mapping private to one page. Do not trigger major implicit actions silently. Do not use pose, YOLO, or classification as the only way to complete a project.
